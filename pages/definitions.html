Chemical Fungi Parts

  • Amatoxins - Found in Amanita, Galerina and Lepiota mushrooms, as well as Conocybe filaris. Cause acute liver and renal failure, cause around 95% of mushroom deaths, according to the NIH.
  • Gyrocyanin -
  • Isoxizole toxins -
  • Ibotenic Acid -
  • Muscimol - Primary psychoactive compound found in A. muscaria and other related mushrooms. Hallucinogenic and classified as a depressant.
  • Decarboxylation - Chemical reaction in which a carboxyl group and CO2 is released.
  • Chitin - Heat-stable polymer that makes up the cell wall of mushrooms. Contains N-acetylglucosamine sugar. The polymer is also found in the exoskeletons of crustaceans and some insects.

Chemical Fungi Testing

  • FeSO4 - Ferrous sulfate, Iron(II) sulfate, or Iron salts. Used to test for color changes, usually in Boletes and Russulas.
  • KOH - Potassium hydroxide, or caustic potash. Strong base. Used to test for color changes, usually in Agaricus, Boletes, and Cortinatius species.
  • NH4OH - Ammonium hydroxide. A weak colorless base often found in cleaning solutions. Used to test for color changes, usually in Boletes.
