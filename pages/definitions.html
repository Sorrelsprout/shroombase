Visible Fungi Parts

  • Hyphae - Strands of the building blocks of fungus.
  • Hyphal Knot - Hyphae condenses and “knots together”. Start of Primordia stage
  • Mycelium - Collection of hyphae.
  • Primordia - After hyphal knots, tiny mini versions of mature mushrooms are formed all over the mycelium.
  • Fruiting body - The mushrooms that we see. The most successful primordia form these, and will eventually be able to release spores to reproduce.
  • Sclerotia - Hardened and compacted mycelium, which contain food reserves. Thicker than ordinary hyphae.

Fungi Descriptions

  • Convoluted - Twisted, contorted, or rolled up. Can be with C. pulchricaerulea in the specimen on the right.
  • Furfuraceous - Covered in bran-like scales.
  • Rosecomb - Deformity where pore-releasing surfaces are present on the top cap of a mushroom, caused by damaged genetics. Generally edible specimens are still safe to eat. However, deformities are often from roadside vehichle fumes and leaks, or cleaning chemicals, so be wary of ingesting specimens too close to populated areas.

Chemical Fungi Testing

  • FeSO4 - Ferrous sulfate, Iron(II) sulfate, or Iron salts. Used to test for color changes, usually in Boletes and Russulas. In this resource, iron tablets are washed to get rid of its exterior coating, and the tablet is then measured and put in a water solution.
  • KOH - Potassium hydroxide, or caustic potash. Strong base. Used to test for color changes, usually in Agaricus, Boletes, and Cortinatius species.
  • Melzer's reagent - Solution of iodine (0.75-1.25%), and potassium iodide (2.50-3.75%), with remaining percentage being 50% chloral hydrate and 50% water.
  • NH3 - Ammonium hydroxide. Also considered Nh4OH in solution, but the -OH is very insignificant unless there is not a lot of solution. A weak colorless base often found in cleaning solutions. Used to test for color changes, usually in Boletes.

Chemical Terms

  • Reagent - Substance or mixture that's used in chemical testing.
  • Solution - Mixture of solute(s) dissolved in solvent.
    • Solute - Substance that gets dissolved
    • Solvent - Substance that does the dissolving, usually a liquid, and often is water
